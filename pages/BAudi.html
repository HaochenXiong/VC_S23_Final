< Baldwin Auditorium

The auditorium was built in 1927 and was originally known as the Womens College Auditorium. It was re-named after Mary Alice Baldwin in 1968. She was the dean of women at Trinity College from 1923 - 1930 and later the first dean of the Women's College from 1930-1947.

Situated at the end of the quad, the building was built to serve as the focal point for the new Women's Campus. Along with the rest of the East Campus buildings being constructed during this time period, the auditorium was designed by the Office of Horace Trumbauer in the Georgian style and constructed with red brick and Vermont marble.

< Duke Chapel

In regards to his new university, J.B. Duke stated: “I want the central building to be a great towering church which will dominate all of the surrounding buildings, because such an edifice would be bound to have a profound influence on the spiritual life of the young men and women who come here.”

The cornerstone for the chapel was laid in 1930, but it wasn't finished until 1932, the last of the original buildings to be completed on West Campus. Along with the rest of West Campus, the chapel was built in the English Gothic style and designed by African American architect Julian Abele in the office of Horace Trumbaeaur.

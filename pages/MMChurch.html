< Duke Memorial Methodist Church

With the rapid growth of population the congregation at Main St. Methodist church needed larger space. In 1906, the congregation acquired Blackwell's house and property and began working on the new church, Memorial Methodist in 1907. The church held its first service in 1912. In 1925, the church was renamed to Duke Memorial Methodist, in honor of its primary benefactor, Washington Duke.

William T. Blackwell (1839-1904)

William T. Blackwell was a tobacco manufacturer born in Person County, North Carolina. During the Civil War, he hired a substitute rather than serving in the military himself. In the 1860s, he began manufacturing and selling tobacco, peddling his products through Eastern North Carolina. Blackwell partnered with James R. Day and John R. Green, after seeing success in Green's tobacco sale amongst the Confederate and Union forces arriving in North Carolina during the Civil War. After the death of Green in 1869, Day and Blackwell purchased the business. In 1886, Blackwell sold his interest in the company and opened a bank, the Bank of Durham, yet his over-generous lending led him to bankruptcy in 1888. After the failure of the bank, Blackwell's assets were mostly eliminated, he continued to live on West Chapel Hill St.

< Friedl Building

The Friedl Building, originally the Science Building, was completed in 1927 and designed by the Office of Horace Trumbaeaur. It was one of the classroom buildings on the main quad of the Women's Campus and housed classrooms, laboratories, offices, and a lecture hall. Like the rest of the campus, it was built in the Georgian style. In 2008, the building was renamed after Ernestine Friedl, the first female dean of the Trinity College of Arts and Sciences.
